Online World

This unit is all about understanding how the internet and online technologies work and how they are used in real life. It covers the technical side of how information travels online, the services that make up the online world, and the impact of the internet on individuals, businesses, and society. It also looks at how to stay safe when using online systems.

This is an exam-based unit.

What You'll Learn:

1. How the Internet Works

  • What makes the internet function — including IP addresses, domain names, and servers.
  • How data is broken down into packets and sent using packet switching.
  • Understanding the role of ISPs (Internet Service Providers), routers, and switches.

2. Online Services

  • Email and instant messaging
  • Social media
  • Cloud computing and storage
  • E-commerce (online shopping)
  • Online education and learning platforms
  • Streaming services (like YouTube and Netflix)
  • How individuals and businesses use these services for communication, entertainment, and collaboration.

3. Data Exchange

  • How data travels across the internet.
  • Understanding the difference between download and upload speeds.
  • Compression and formats used for transferring files.

4. Security and Safety

  • Dangers like viruses, hacking, phishing, and malware.
  • How to protect yourself with:
    • Strong passwords
    • Two-factor authentication
    • Antivirus software
    • Firewalls and encryption
  • Importance of following acceptable use policies and staying safe online.

5. Impact of the Online World

  • How the internet has changed:
    • Communication
    • Business and commerce
    • Education and training
    • Social relationships
  • Positive and negative effects, including digital addiction and job automation.

Assessment

1-hour online exam with multiple-choice and short-answer questions.
Focuses on your knowledge and understanding of how the online world functions.
